How to Use AI Tools Responsibly as a Student

✅ Do: Use AI as a starting point
AI-generated content is a first draft. Edit it, add your own examples, your own voice, your own analysis. Academic submissions should be reviewed and personalised before submitting.
✅ Do: Use AI to learn faster
Ask AI to explain a concept 3 different ways. Ask it to quiz you on a topic. Ask it to show you a real-world example. That's a legitimate study accelerator.
⚠️ Always: Fact-check AI outputs
AI tools can confidently state incorrect information. For anything you'll cite in a report, submit to a professor, or base a decision on — verify with an authoritative source.
⚠️ Be aware: Most universities have AI policies
Many Indian universities now have guidelines on AI use in submissions. Know your institution's policy before submitting AI-assisted work.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these AI tools free for students?
Yes — all tools on Forjit AI are free. The calculators (CGPA, Age, Percentage, Salary, etc.) are always free with no API key. The AI tools (resume builder, study planner, app generator) are free when you use your Groq API key — available free at console.groq.com.
Can I use Forjit AI for my engineering or MBA final project?
The AI App Generator can help you build functional HTML/JavaScript applications as part of mini-projects, prototypes, and demos. Use the generated code as a starting point — review it, understand it, and customise it. Always follow your institution's guidelines on AI-assisted projects.
How does the CGPA to percentage conversion work?
Different universities use different conversion formulas. Common ones: Percentage = CGPA × 9.5 (Anna University / most engineering colleges), Percentage = CGPA × 10 (some universities), or use the actual marks-based calculation. Our CGPA calculator covers all major formats.
Which government exams have age limits I should track?
UPSC Civil Services (21–32 years), SSC CGL (18–32), IBPS PO (20–30), SBI PO (21–30), RBI Grade B (21–30), GATE (no age limit), CAT/XAT/GMAT (no age limit). Use the Age Calculator to check your exact eligibility against any exam's cut-off date.
