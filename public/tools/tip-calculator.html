Tipping Culture in India

Tipping is customary but not mandatory in India. Unlike the USA where 15–20% tips are expected, Indian tipping customs are more informal and the amounts are smaller. Here is a general guide to tipping in different situations in India:

  • Restaurants (sit-down, mid-range): 5–10% of the bill. Many restaurants add a service charge (5–10%) automatically — check before tipping extra.
  • Restaurants (fine dining): 10% if service charge is not included. Verify on the bill first.
  • Hotel housekeeping: ₹50–100 per day for budget hotels, ₹100–200 for luxury hotels.
  • Taxi / cab (Ola, Uber): Not mandatory — the app handles payment. A small cash tip of ₹20–50 is appreciated for long rides.
  • Food delivery (Swiggy, Zomato): ₹10–30 is appreciated, especially during rain or late night.
  • Salon / spa: ₹50–100 for the service person, not the cashier.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is tipping mandatory in Indian restaurants?
No, tipping is not mandatory in India. However, a 5–10% tip is customary in mid-range and fine dining restaurants. Always check your bill first — many restaurants automatically add a service charge, making an additional tip unnecessary.
